Mechanics

Vectors, kinematics, Newton's laws, connected particles, moments, and energy — the mathematical treatment of motion and forces.

01

Vectors

Vector Notation & Components
a = xi + yj = (x, y)
Component Form
i and j are unit vectors along the x and y axes. x = horizontal component, y = vertical component.
|a| = √(x² + y²)
Magnitude
The length of the vector — always positive
θ = tan⁻¹(y / x)
Direction
Angle measured from the positive x-axis (i-direction)
a + b = (x₁+x₂)i + (y₁+y₂)j
Vector Addition
Add components separately
â = a / |a|
Unit Vector
A vector of magnitude 1 in the same direction as a
|AB| = |b − a|
Distance Between Points
Magnitude of the displacement vector from A to B

02

Kinematics

SUVAT Equations (Straight-Line Motion)
v = u + at
No displacement
s = ut + ½at²
No final velocity
v² = u² + 2as
No time
s = ½(u + v)t
No acceleration
s = vt − ½at²
No initial velocity
Calculus Connections
v = ds/dt
Velocity = rate of change of displacement
a = dv/dt = d²s/dt²
Acceleration = rate of change of velocity
v = ∫a dt
Integrate acceleration to get velocity
Add constant of integration using initial conditions
s = ∫v dt
Integrate velocity to get displacement
Vector Kinematics (2D Motion)
r = r₀ + vt + ½at²
Position Vector
r₀ = initial position vector, v = initial velocity vector, a = acceleration vector — all in component (i, j) form
v = v₀ + at
Velocity Vector
Speed = |v|
Speed from velocity vector
Speed is the magnitude of the velocity vector (scalar)
Graphs of Motion

Displacement-Time (s–t)

Gradient = velocity. A curve indicates changing velocity (acceleration). Horizontal line = object at rest.

Velocity-Time (v–t)

Gradient = acceleration. Area under the graph = displacement. Area below the time-axis represents negative displacement.

Acceleration-Time (a–t)

Area under the graph = change in velocity. Constant a → straight horizontal line.

03

Forces & Newton's Laws

Core Force Equations
F = ma
Newton's Second Law
F = resultant force (N), m = mass (kg), a = acceleration (ms⁻²). In vector form: ΣF = ma
W = mg
Weight
g ≈ 9.8 ms⁻² (CIE uses 9.8 in Mechanics, unlike Physics' 9.81)
ΣF = 0 ⟺ equilibrium
Equilibrium Condition
Object is at rest or moving with constant velocity
F ≤ μR (limiting case: F = μR)
Friction
μ = coefficient of friction, R = normal reaction force. Friction acts to oppose relative motion.
Resolving Forces
Resolve along and perpendicular to the direction of motion (or an inclined plane)
Resolving Strategy
Component along plane: F sinθ or F cosθ depending on the angle's position. Choose axes that simplify the equations — usually along the slope and perpendicular to it.
Along slope: mg sinθ − F = ma
Object on Inclined Plane
θ = angle of incline. F = friction (if present) acting up the slope when object slides down.
Perpendicular: R = mg cosθ
Normal Reaction (on incline)
Used together with F = μR for friction problems
04

Connected Particles

Strings, Pulleys & Tension
Same string ⟹ same tension T throughout (assuming light, inextensible string and smooth/light pulley)
Key Assumption
Both connected particles have the SAME magnitude of acceleration a
For falling mass: m₁g − T = m₁a
Particle 1 (heavier, descending)
For rising mass: T − m₂g = m₂a
Particle 2 (lighter, ascending)
Add the two equations: (m₁ − m₂)g = (m₁ + m₂)a
Solving the System
Eliminates T to find a directly. Substitute back to find T.
For horizontal connected particles: T = m₂a, F − T = m₁a (driving force F)
Particles on a Surface
Adjust signs for friction on each particle if present
05

Moments & Equilibrium

Moments
Moment = F × d
Turning Moment
F = force (N), d = perpendicular distance from pivot to line of action of force (m). Units: Nm
Σ clockwise moments = Σ anticlockwise moments
Principle of Moments
Applies when a rigid body is in rotational equilibrium — take moments about any convenient point (usually a pivot or support)
Equilibrium of Rigid Bodies
For full equilibrium: ΣFₓ = 0, ΣFᵥ = 0, AND Σmoments = 0
Three Conditions
All three must hold simultaneously for an object to be in static equilibrium
x̄ = Σ(mᵢxᵢ) / Σmᵢ
Centre of Mass (system of particles)
Weighted average position. Apply separately for y-coordinate: ȳ = Σ(mᵢyᵢ)/Σmᵢ
Toppling: object topples when line of weight falls outside the base
Toppling Condition
Check if centre of mass remains above the base for stability
06

Work, Energy & Power

Formulas
W = Fs cosθ
Work Done
θ = angle between force and direction of motion. If F is along direction of motion, W = Fs.
KE = ½mv²
Kinetic Energy
GPE = mgh
Gravitational Potential Energy
h = vertical height gained
Work-Energy Principle: Work done by resultant force = ΔKE = ½mv² − ½mu²
Work-Energy Theorem
If non-conservative forces (friction) act: Work done against friction = loss in mechanical energy
P = W / t = Fv
Power
P = power (W), F = driving force (N), v = velocity (ms⁻¹) — for constant velocity
Energy conservation: KE₁ + PE₁ = KE₂ + PE₂ (no friction)
Conservation of Mechanical Energy
Total mechanical energy stays constant if only gravity does work
